03 · ML at Work · Project 01 · Sprout Solutions
01

The AI that learns
how you code.

We're transforming software development at Sprout Solutions so that the majority of the engineering workflow runs through AI agents.

One piece of that: an automatic code reviewer with a learning feedback loop. It watches how developers write code — their patterns, their preferences — and uses that to get smarter at reviewing future code.

The machine doesn't just review. It learns. Every pull request makes it better.

This is what ML looks like at work. Not sci-fi. Just smart automation.
03 · ML at Work · Project 02 · Canadian Startup
02

Building products
at the speed of AI.

We run a micro-SaaS model — small apps, specific niches, minimal monthly fee. The bet is volume: many products, each serving a different group of people.

The problem? How do you launch 10 products a year instead of 1?

We built ML pipelines that pull Google Trends, market research, and competitor data — then AI agents that analyze it all and give us launch recommendations in hours, not months.

Faster decisions. Smarter bets. Less guessing.

ML for business strategy — not just engineering.
03 · ML at Work · Project 03 · Pride Month 2026
03

Teaching a machine
to explain love.

A straight friend asked me a random question about gender and attraction. It stopped me. That one question turned into an app.

We used ML to study the behavioral patterns around SOGIE education — what makes it confusing for people who haven't lived it, and what actually makes it click.

The finding? Gamify it. Make it layman. Make it feel like a conversation, not a lecture.

We built a SOGIE awareness app that spreads understanding through play — designed for people who don't yet know what they don't know.

ML doesn't have to be cold. It can be compassionate.
